From the graph it becomes clear that cars with two driving wheels have a much wider price range than cars with other types of drives.

At the same time, the median price for cars with all-wheel drive is higher than for cars with other types of drives.

Speaking of cars driving on gasoline, you can notice that RHD is much greater variation in price, which is likely due to their large number, the median price is $ 1,000, while the median price of the machine with the left rudder driving on gasoline a few more(about 50 hundred dollars).

Cars with steering wheel on the center use only diesel fuel. The difference in the median price of cars with right and left hand drive driving on diesel fuel is very different (about 400 hundreds of dollars).

Cars driving on other types of fuel are represented only with right hand drive

Almost all cars with two driving wheels are equipped with an automatic transmission, only a small part of all cars has a manual transmission less than 100 has a cvt transmission.

A similar situation is developing with cars with all-wheel drive. Most of all cars with an automatic transmission, cars with a manual transmission are about three times less, and cars with a cvt type transmission are not represented at all
